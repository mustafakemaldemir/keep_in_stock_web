These are the most common questions for a private, multi-location inventory setup.

Getting started

Create locations that match real storage areas such as kitchen, office, pantry, or storage room. This keeps product totals easier to trust later.

Use the ready-made catalog for products you buy often. It saves setup time and helps you start tracking inventory right away.

Daily usage

Record both stock in and stock out movements whenever quantities change. The movement history screen helps you verify what affected the current total.

When you mark a prepared list as completed, the list quantities can be added back into the selected location so restocking stays fast.

Keep in Stock uses the limit and daily decrease values you define to estimate remaining days and schedule local notifications accordingly.

Privacy and data

Your data is intended to stay on your device. The app does not rely on an account system or built-in cloud backend to manage your inventory.

No. Keep in Stock is positioned as a privacy-focused app with no account wall, no analytics dashboard, and no advertising trackers built in.

Notification permission allows the app to alert you locally when stock is predicted to run low. If notifications are disabled, inventory tracking still works but reminders will not appear.
